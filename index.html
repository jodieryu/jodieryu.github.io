experiences
Student Intern | iXperience

Two summer's ago I spent 8 unforgettable weeks in Cape Town, South Africa -- a country I never imagined to find myself in. The first four weeks I was immersed in an intensive front-end web development class, in which I learned high demand coding skills, such as HTML/CSS, Javascript, AngularJS and other computing concepts such as how to use databases and APIs. The last four weeks I was able to implement these skills while interning at a local company called Pendulum Advisors. There, I developed and designed a completely new web platform from plain HTML/CSS which included backend functionalities such as a client login and uploading storage into a cloud database. Not only did I learn so much, but I also had the opportunity to meet passionate, hardworking, and kind people who shared my love in adventure and learning.

Barista | Caffe Amouri

Ever since I was a young girl, I have had a strong curiosity and fascination in coffee. Waking up to the bittersweet scent, and watching my father run out the door with a mug of it, always caught my interest. Since then, I not only wanted to learn how to make great coffee, but to learn more about its history, its art, and its community. After working at a small batch roaster called Caffe Amouri near my house, I not only learned more about coffee, but I also learned how to provide customers with the best coffee experience. I loved putting smiles on the faces of regulars when I remembered their name and their orders, and I also loved working and learning about my team. From working here, I not only gained great experience in organization, communication, teamwork, and discipline... but also in making wonderful personal connections.

Treasurer | Vietnamese Student Association

VSA at UVA is a cultural, social, philanthropic, and sports organization aiming to educate others about Vietnamese culture while also establishing strong relationships among its members and with other organizations at UVA. Our events cover a variety of these goals from sharing the Vietnamese culture, raising money for various philanthropies, giving career and post-graduate guidance to our members, and reaching out to the greater UVA and Charlottesville communities. As treasurer, I ensure that the organization has suitable funding by applying and participating in budget hearings from resources such as UVA's Student Council, Parent's Committee, and more. I also manage the financial accounts and allocate money for the different events we sponsor, one of our biggest being our annual Color Me Cured 5K. Although I am not Vietnamese, I have found a truly caring and loving family within the members of VSA.

Event Coordinator | First Year Leadership Experience

FYLE at UVA is a program that helps First Year students foster growth in their residential and class communities through the core values of academic rigor, diversity and multiculturalism, health and wellness, honor, public service and student self-governance. As part of the representative body of students in their first academic year, my role as Event Coordinator was to plan and create events that would bring my residential association together. Some of these events included Open Mic night, Game night, and other bonding activities that built a sense of community within our class.
